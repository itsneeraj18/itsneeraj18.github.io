Motor control refers to the regulation and manipulation of electric motors to achieve desired mechanical output. It encompasses everything from simple on/off switching to sophisticated algorithms that optimize efficiency, torque, and speed across various applications.

In electric vehicles, motor control is the "brain" that translates driver inputs into precise motor commands, ensuring smooth acceleration, regenerative braking, and optimal energy efficiency.

üîÑ Open Loop V/f Control

Theory & Fundamentals

Open-loop control is often used during startup or in low-cost motor drives where position sensors are not available. In PMSM and BLDC motors, the controller generates voltage waveforms that force the rotor to follow a rotating magnetic field.

The motor is driven using voltage-frequency (V/f) control along with a controlled ramp in electrical angle. The inverter produces phase voltages using techniques like SPWM to approximate sinusoidal waveforms.

Before closed-loop Field Oriented Control (FOC) can take over, open-loop alignment and acceleration ensure the rotor is synchronized with the stator field without stalling or excessive current.
