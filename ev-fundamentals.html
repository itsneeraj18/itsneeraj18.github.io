ğŸš— Overview: What Makes EVs Different?

Electric vehicles represent a fundamental shift in automotive architecture. Unlike internal combustion engines (ICEs) with their complex mechanical transmissions, EVs feature simplified drivetrains powered by high-voltage battery systems, electric motors, and sophisticated power electronics.

This domain covers the foundational concepts you need to understand EV systems: from basic powertrain architecture to advanced high-voltage management strategies.

Key EV Subsystems

  • High-Voltage Battery: 200-400V lithium-ion packs with BMS
  • Motor & Inverter: Propulsion motor and power conversion
  • Thermal Management: Battery and motor cooling systems
  • Charging Infrastructure: AC and DC charging systems
  • Safety Systems: HV isolation, fault detection, emergency shutdown
