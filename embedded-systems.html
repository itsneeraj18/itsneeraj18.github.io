Embedded systems are the "brains" of modern EVs, managing motor control, battery systems, thermal management, and vehicle safety in real-time. These systems must operate with extreme reliability, often in harsh thermal and electromagnetic environments.

This domain covers microcontroller programming, real-time operating systems, and advanced control algorithms that power today's electric vehicles.

Core Embedded Systems in EVs

  • Motor Controller: High-frequency PWM and FOC algorithms
  • BMS ECU: Cell monitoring and balancing logic
  • Vehicle Controller: Torque request coordination
  • Charging Controller: Power management during AC/DC charging
  • Thermal Manager: Temperature-based fan and pump control
