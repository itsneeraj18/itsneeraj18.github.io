Renewable energy systems convert natural resources (solar, wind, geothermal) into electrical power while maintaining grid stability and efficiency. Modern renewable systems require sophisticated control electronics to manage intermittency, voltage regulation, and power quality.

This domain focuses on grid-connected and standalone renewable systems, with emphasis on control strategies that enable seamless integration with EV infrastructure and microgrids.

Key Components

  • Photovoltaic Arrays: Solar panels generating DC power
  • Power Conversion: DC-DC and DC-AC inverters
  • Grid Interface: Synchronization and protection systems
  • Energy Storage: Battery systems for load balancing
  • Control Systems: Real-time monitoring and optimization
